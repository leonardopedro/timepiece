\begin{abstract}
We describe, and partly formalize in Lean~4, a strategy for the Riemann
Hypothesis that does not attempt to prove ``no zero off the line'' analytically.
Instead it encodes RH as a single \(\Pi^0_1\) arithmetical sentence --- Schoenfeld's
(1976) effective prime-counting bound, made primitive recursive --- and shows that
a \emph{counterexample} to that sentence would have to be \emph{detectable} by a
regular conditional probability (rcp) kernel built from a random Dirichlet series
\(\sum_n X_n n^{-s}\) over an atomless prior, with \textbf{bounded coefficients}
(\(\|X_n\|\le 1\)) --- critical, as this is what gives absolute convergence on
\(\Rea(s)>1\) and so anchors the random series to \(\eta\). The detectability is then
ruled out: conditioning on a
\emph{local} edge neighborhood of \(\eta\) (never a contour enclosing the suspected
zero \(s_0\), which the maximum-modulus principle would forbid), \textbf{Bagchi/Voronin
universality} provides a positive-mass family of zero-free realizations that match
\(\eta\) on the edges yet stay nonzero at \(s_0\). So the rcp of a zero at \(s_0\) is
\(\neq 1\): a zero cannot be forced to conditional-probability \(1\), i.e.\ cannot be
\emph{proved} in the model. The contrapositive is that no counterexample is provable,
so the \(\Pi^0_1\) Schoenfeld sentence holds, and (optionally, via Schoenfeld's
analytic equivalence) RH.

The probabilistic substrate is an instance of the \textbf{Kopperman formalism} used
in the companion \(P \neq NP\) development: a separable Hilbert space, a decidable
dense skeleton of finite Dirichlet approximants, and an \emph{atomless} Mehler-type
measure specified by its mean and covariance. The non-detectability is the same
``atomlessness does the work'' phenomenon read one level down the arithmetical
hierarchy --- \(\Pi^0_1\) here versus \(\Pi^0_2\) for \(P\neq NP\) --- and is honestly
an \emph{unprovability} statement: the rcp of a strip zero is \(\neq 1\), so
``\(\eta\) has a zero at \(s_0\)'' has no witness in the \(\mathrm{RCA}_0/\Pi^0_2\)
model, leaving the \(\Pi^0_1\) Schoenfeld sentence with no disproof. The route has
\textbf{two} deep, un-formalized analytic inputs: \textbf{Schoenfeld (1976)} (the
bridge) and \textbf{Bagchi (1981) universality} (non-detection).

In Lean, the probabilistic core \texttt{RcpEuler.lean} (Phases~0--2: the random
product, the atomless prior, the rcp kernel) is \textbf{\texttt{sorry}-free}; but the
on-disk non-detectability lemma \texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt} is proved only for a trivial
\(\forall k\) form of the rcp-zero predicate (a \emph{placeholder}) --- the
substantive limit form, powered by universality, is not yet discharged. The
arithmetical layer \texttt{SchoenfeldPRA.lean} (Phases~3--5) carries \textbf{four}
\texttt{sorry}s: the primitive-recursive Schoenfeld matrix and its \texttt{Primrec}
proof, the load-bearing bridge \texttt{counterexample\_iff\_rcpZero}, and the optional
analytic return \texttt{riemann\_hypothesis\_via\_rcp}. The result is a \emph{verified
reduction} of RH to those statements (plus the limit-form non-detection), not a
complete proof.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Strategy: encode, then fail to detect}

The classical attack on RH tries to show directly that \(\zeta\) has no zero in the
open right half \(\{1/2 < \Rea(s) < 1\}\) of the critical strip. The present
strategy replaces that analytic goal by a two-move \emph{logical} one.

\paragraph{Move 1 --- encode RH as a \(\Pi^0_1\) sentence.} Schoenfeld (1976) proved
that, \emph{assuming} RH, the prime-counting function satisfies the explicit bound
\begin{equation}\label{eq:schoenfeld}
  \bigl|\pi(n) - \operatorname{li}(n)\bigr|
  \;\le\; \frac{1}{8\pi}\,\sqrt{n}\,\ln n
  \qquad (n \ge 2657),
\end{equation}
and conversely this family of bounds is equivalent to RH. Squaring to remove the
square root and truncating \(\ln\), \(\operatorname{li}\) to a predictable rational
precision turns \eqref{eq:schoenfeld} into a \textbf{primitive recursive} predicate
\(\texttt{schoenfeld}(n) \in \{\texttt{true},\texttt{false}\}\). RH then becomes the
single \(\Pi^0_1\) arithmetical sentence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhpra}
  \texttt{RH\_PRA} \;\equiv\; \forall n,\ \texttt{schoenfeld}(n + 2657) = \texttt{true}.
\end{equation}
A \emph{counterexample} is a number \(n\) with
\(\texttt{schoenfeld}(n+2657)=\texttt{false}\); RH is the assertion that none exists.

\paragraph{Move 2 --- show a counterexample would be probabilistically detectable,
then deny detection.} We attach to the strip a random Euler product \(\eulerB\) over
a prior \(\mu\), and a \emph{regular conditional probability} kernel that asks: given
that \(\eulerB\) looks like \(\eta\) on the boundary of a small rectangle, what is the
conditional law of \(\eulerB\) at an interior point \(s_0\)? A genuine zero of
\(\eta\) at \(s_0\) would force this conditional law to put nonvanishing mass near
\(0\) --- an \emph{rcp-zero} (Definition~\ref{def:rcpzero}). The bridge
(Section~\ref{sec:bridge}) says a Schoenfeld counterexample produces exactly such an
rcp-zero. The non-detectability lemma (Section~\ref{sec:nondetect}) says no rcp-zero
exists. Composing the two:
\[
  (\exists\,\text{counterexample}) \;\Longrightarrow\; (\exists\,\text{rcp-zero})
  \;\Longrightarrow\; \texttt{False},
\]
so \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) holds, and (optionally, via Schoenfeld's equivalence) RH
follows.

\subsection{Why ``regular conditional probability'' and not a single condition}

The kernel is a genuine \emph{disintegration} (Lean
\texttt{ProbabilityTheory.condDistrib}), not conditioning on one positive-measure
event (\texttt{ProbabilityTheory.cond}). This matters because the event ``\(\eulerB\)
equals \(\eta\) on the boundary'' is null: the boundary trace pins a continuum of
constraints. Regular conditional probability is precisely the tool that assigns a
well-defined conditional law to such null conditioning data, via Tjur's
neighborhood-limit characterization
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tjur}
  \rcp(A \mid T = t)
  \;=\; \lim_{V \downarrow \{T=t\}} \frac{\mathbb{P}(A \cap V)}{\mathbb{P}(V)},
\end{equation}
the limit over shrinking open neighborhoods \(V\) of the trace value. The limit is
well defined because the substrate is \textbf{Radon} (a Polish inner-product space
has inner-regular Borel measures), and it is this limit, not a naive ratio, that the
non-detectability lemma controls.

\section{The Bounded-Coefficient Prior and the rcp Kernel}

\subsection{The model}\label{sec:model}

Fix a cutoff \(P\) and a sample \(\omega \in \Omega_b = \mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{C}\)
recording one complex coefficient per prime index.

\begin{definition}[Bounded exponential Euler product]\label{def:eulerB}
With \(\eta^E_P(s) = (1-2^{1-s})\prod_{p\le P}(1-p^{-s})^{-1}\) the deterministic
eta--Euler approximant and \(\texttt{cCorr}(s)=\log\eta^E_P(s)\) the centering, set
\[
  X_p(\omega) = \omega_p, \qquad
  \texttt{bSum}_P(s,\omega) = \sum_{\substack{p\le P\\ p\ \mathrm{prime}}} X_p(\omega)\,p^{-s},
  \qquad
  \eulerB_P(s,\omega) = \exp\bigl(\texttt{bSum}_P(s,\omega) + \texttt{cCorr}(s)\bigr).
\]
In Lean these are \texttt{Xb}, \texttt{bSum}, \texttt{eulerB} in
\texttt{RcpEuler.lean}.
\end{definition}

Two structural facts hold with \emph{no} hypothesis on \(s\):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \(\eulerB_P(\cdot,\omega) \ne 0\) everywhere (an exponential is never zero)
        \textbf{(proved: \texttt{eulerB\_ne\_zero})}, so the reciprocal
        \(1/\eulerB\) needed by the contour side has no spurious poles;
  \item \(\eulerB_P(\cdot,\omega)\) is analytic on the strip
        \textbf{(proved: \texttt{eulerB\_differentiableOn})}.
\end{itemize}
The decisive difference from the Gaussian model is the \textbf{boundedness} of the
coefficients: when \(\|X_p(\omega)\|\le 1\), the tail
\(\sum_{p>P}X_p(\omega)p^{-s}\) is dominated by \(\sum_p p^{-a}\) on
\(\{\Rea(s)\ge a>1\}\) for \emph{every} \(\omega\), giving absolute, locally uniform
convergence with \emph{no} almost-sure qualifier
\textbf{(proved: \texttt{bSum\_tail\_small}, a Weierstrass \(M\)-test)}.

\begin{definition}[The atomless bounded prior]\label{def:prior}
Let \(\texttt{diskLaw}\) be the uniform probability law on the closed unit disk
\(\overline{\mathbb{D}}\subset\mathbb{C}\), and
\[
  \texttt{priorBall} \;=\; \bigotimes_{p}\ \texttt{diskLaw}
  \;=\; \texttt{Measure.infinitePi}\,(\lambda\_.\ \texttt{diskLaw})
\]
the independent product over prime indices. It is a probability measure
\textbf{(proved: \texttt{priorBall\_isProb})}, supported in the closed unit ball
\textbf{(proved: \texttt{priorBall\_ball}, so \(\|X_p\|\le 1\) a.e.)}, and
\textbf{atomless} \textbf{(proved: \texttt{priorBall\_atomless},
\(\texttt{priorBall}\{\omega\}=0\))}.
\end{definition}

Atomlessness is the load-bearing structural property: it is exactly what prevents
any single realization --- in particular the deterministic \(\eta\)-realization
\(X_p\equiv 1\) --- from carrying positive mass, so detection of a zero must come
from a \emph{neighborhood}, where the conditional behaviour can be controlled.

\subsection{The general model: mean and covariance}\label{sec:general}

Nothing in the argument uses the Euler product as such. The substrate may be taken to
be \emph{any} random Dirichlet series \(\sum_n X_n n^{-s}\) with \textbf{uniformly
bounded coefficients} \(\|X_n\|\le 1\), specified --- Mehler/Kopperman-style --- by two
moments:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{mean} \(\mathbb{E}[X_n]=1\): the \(X_n\equiv 1\) realization is the
        deterministic target series, used as the conditioning center;
  \item \textbf{covariance} \(\mathbb{E}\bigl[(X-\text{mean})^2\bigr]\): the chosen law.
\end{itemize}
``Choosing the law is choosing the model.'' The bounded prior of \S\ref{sec:model} is
one admissible covariance; the Fock-space picture (first \(P\) coefficients pinned, the
rest free) is another. The conclusion is a property of \((\text{mean},\text{covariance})\),
not of any product structure --- and, by Remark~\ref{rem:nomult}, the coefficients need
not be multiplicative.

\emph{Boundedness is not optional, but multiplicativity is.} The bound \(\|X_n\|\le 1\)
is \textbf{critical}: it gives \emph{absolute}, locally uniform convergence on
\(\{\Rea(s)\ge a>1\}\) for \emph{every} \(\omega\) (a Weierstrass \(M\)-test against
\(\sum_n n^{-a}\), \texttt{bSum\_tail\_small}), which is exactly what anchors the random
series to the genuine \(\eta\) --- whose Dirichlet series converges there. Without it the
series need not converge for every \(\omega\) on \(\Rea>1\), and nothing about \(\eta\)
could be concluded. Inside the strip, \(1/2<\Rea(s)\le 1\), absolute convergence fails;
there the \(X_n\) being uncorrelated (independent under the product prior) with bounded
variance makes the centered series' term-variances \(\operatorname{Var}(X_n)\,n^{-2\Rea(s)}\)
summable for \(\Rea(s)>1/2\) --- \textbf{Kolmogorov's one-series theorem} (\(L^2\) from
orthogonality, almost-sure via the \(L^2\)-bounded martingale of partial sums). These two
regimes --- absolute on \(\Rea>1\), Kolmogorov on \(\Rea>1/2\) --- are the right-edge and
left-edge regimes of the contour, and both ingredients are already in the project: the
deterministic Dirichlet-series summability, locally-uniform convergence, and analyticity
on \(\{\Rea>1/2\}\) (\texttt{EtaConvergence.lean}: \texttt{summable\_etaPairedTerm},
\texttt{paired\_tendstoUniformlyOn}, \texttt{analyticOnNhd\_paired\_tsum}), and the
i.i.d.\ Mehler product with its independence and variance (\texttt{SphereGaussian.lean}:
\texttt{gammaMeasure}, \texttt{iIndepFun\_infinitePi}, \texttt{variance\_id\_gaussianReal}).

\subsection{The rcp kernel}\label{sec:kernel}

Let \(\partial^\ast R = R.\texttt{closure}\setminus R.\texttt{openInt}\) be the
topological boundary of a rectangle \(R\). Write the \emph{edge trace}
\(\texttt{edgeTrace}_P(\omega) = (z\mapsto \eulerB_P(z,\omega))\restriction
\partial^\ast R\) and the \emph{interior value}
\(\texttt{interiorVal}_P(s_0,\omega)=\eulerB_P(s_0,\omega)\).

\begin{definition}[rcp kernel]\label{def:kernel}
\[
  \texttt{rcpKernel}_P(s_0,R)
  \;=\; \texttt{condDistrib}\bigl(\texttt{interiorVal}_P(s_0,\cdot),\ \texttt{edgeTrace}_P,\ \texttt{priorBall}\bigr),
\]
the regular conditional distribution of the interior value given the edge trace,
under the bounded prior. \textbf{(Lean: \texttt{rcpKernel}, built from
\texttt{condDistrib}; measurability of both maps and finiteness of
\texttt{priorBall} are the inputs.)}
\end{definition}

The kernel is well posed because the precondition of Tjur's
characterization~\eqref{eq:tjur} holds: every neighborhood of the \(\eta\)-trace ---
measured against the deterministic skeleton \(\eta^E_P\) on which \(\eulerB\) centers
--- has \emph{positive} prior mass \textbf{(proved:
\texttt{priorBall\_edgeNbhd\_pos}, from the positive-mass coefficient box
\texttt{priorBall\_box\_pos} and \(\|\exp(\cdot)-1\|\) control)}. This is the honest,
\emph{true} restatement of an earlier dubious comparison against the exact \(\eta\):
small-coefficient concentration only yields closeness to the \emph{approximant}
\(\eta^E_P\), and that is exactly what positivity-of-support needs.

\subsection{The contour side, for free}

On the boundary, the reciprocals converge: if a sequence
\(\eulerB_{P_k}(\cdot,\omega_k)\to\eta\) uniformly on \(\partial^\ast R\) (with
\(\eta\) zero-free there, i.e.\ its only strip zero is the interior \(s_0\)), then
\[
  \oint_{\partial R}\frac{dz}{\eulerB_{P_k}(z,\omega_k)}
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \oint_{\partial R}\frac{dz}{\eta(z)} .
\]
\textbf{(Proved: \texttt{rcp\_recipEulerB\_tendsto\_recipEta}, via a per-edge
dominated-convergence step \texttt{edge\_integral\_tendsto} and continuity helpers
\texttt{etaRect\_continuousOn}, \texttt{eulerB\_continuousOn}, \dots; the strip
hypothesis \(\Rea<1\) on the closure is what makes the integrands continuous.)}
Because each \(1/\eulerB\) is entire on \(\overline{R}\) (Definition~\ref{def:eulerB}),
each finite contour integral vanishes; the limit transfers vanishing to
\(\oint_{\partial R}1/\eta\). This is the same ``exponential \(\Rightarrow\) free
Cauchy'' mechanism as before, now used only as supporting infrastructure for the
probabilistic statement.

\subsection{Non-detectability}\label{sec:nondetect}

Condition on the values on a \emph{local} neighborhood of the boundary --- never on a
closed contour enclosing \(s_0\) (the reason is Remark~\ref{rem:maxmod}). Write \(T\)
for that local edge-trace and \(e_\eta\) for the trace of \(\eta\).

\begin{definition}[rcp-zero, limit form]\label{def:rcpzero}
\(\eta\) has an \emph{rcp-zero} at \(s_0\) if the regular conditional probability
\[
  L \;=\; \rcp\bigl(\,\text{interior value at }s_0 = 0 \ \big|\ T = e_\eta\,\bigr)
  \;=\; \lim_{V\downarrow\{T=e_\eta\}} \frac{\mathbb{P}\bigl(\{\text{interior}=0\}\cap V\bigr)}{\mathbb{P}(V)}
\]
equals \(1\): conditioned on matching \(\eta\) on the local edge data, the interior
value is forced to \(0\) with certainty. The Tjur limit exists because the substrate
is Radon.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[The on-disk \(\forall k\) form is a placeholder]\label{rem:placeholder}
The Lean \texttt{rcpZeroAt} currently quantifies positive joint mass \emph{at every}
cutoff \(k\) (including a fixed smallest \(k=0\)):
\(\forall r>0,\ \forall k,\ \texttt{priorBall}\{\text{edge-close within }\varepsilon_k
\wedge \|\eulerB_{P_k}(s_0,\cdot)\|<r\}>0\). At a fixed cutoff the finite product
\(\eulerB_{P_0}(s_0,\cdot)\) is bounded below by a constant \(r_0>0\) on the prior
support, so that event is null and the \(\forall k\) statement is
\emph{unconditionally false} --- whence \texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt} is dispatched in one
line by taking \(k=0\). That pair carries no information about \(\eta\)'s zeros and
pushes all content onto the bridge; it is a placeholder. The substantive object is the
\emph{eventual} (\(P_k\to\infty\)) Tjur-limit form of Definition~\ref{def:rcpzero}.
\end{remark}

Under the limit form a true zero \emph{is} visible, through the centering:
\(\Rea\,\texttt{cCorr}_P(s_0)=\log|\eta^E_P(s_0)|\to\log|\eta(s_0)|\), so if
\(\eta(s_0)=0\) the interior modulus is driven to \(0\) on mass \(\to 1\) at large
\(k\). Limit-form non-detectability is therefore \emph{not} automatic; it is exactly:

\begin{theorem}[No rcp-zero: \(L\neq 1\)]\label{thm:nondetect}
Conditioned on a \emph{local} edge neighborhood of \(\eta\) that does not enclose
\(s_0\), the conditional law of the interior value is not a point mass at \(0\):
\(L\neq 1\).
\end{theorem}
The mechanism is \textbf{Bagchi/Voronin universality}. Among the realizations
consistent with the local edge data there is a positive-mass subset --- an
\emph{alternative average} --- given by a \emph{zero-free} random Dirichlet series
that matches \(\eta\) on the edges but stays nonzero at \(s_0\); universality is
precisely the statement that the prior's support is all zero-free holomorphic
functions on a connected-complement compact, so such a subset exists and keeps
\(L<1\). The analytic input is Bagchi (1981); the closest formal statement available
is the universality lemma \texttt{eulerG\_universality\_threeLeftEdges} of the
companion file. \textbf{The on-disk \texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt} (the \(r_0\) one-liner of
Remark~\ref{rem:placeholder}) is a placeholder for this; the limit form is a genuine,
not-yet-discharged load-bearer.}

\begin{remark}[Local neighborhood, and why not the full boundary]\label{rem:maxmod}
The conditioning must be on a \emph{local} neighborhood of a boundary point --- no
particular shape or size, only that it does not enclose \(s_0\). On the \emph{full}
rectangle boundary the maximum-modulus principle would force any holomorphic
realization \(\varepsilon\)-close to \(\eta\) on \(\partial R\) to be
\(\varepsilon\)-close to \(\eta\) throughout \(R\), hence \(|f(s_0)|<\varepsilon\) ---
pinning the interior to \(\eta(s_0)=0\) and giving \(L=1\). A local, non-enclosing
neighborhood escapes the pin, and is exactly the connected-complement geometry that
universality requires.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The conclusion is unprovability, not absence of a zero]\label{rem:unprov}
\(L\neq 1\) says a zero at \(s_0\) \emph{cannot be forced to conditional-probability
\(1\)} --- it cannot be \emph{proved} in the model --- not that \(\eta(s_0)\neq 0\).
Via the bridge and the \(\Sigma^0_1\) witness property (\S\ref{sec:witness}), this
yields: no Schoenfeld counterexample is provable, so the \(\Pi^0_1\) sentence
\(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) has no disproof. The step from there to ``\(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) is
\emph{true}'' is the model/\(T\)-conservativity reading of \S\ref{sec:unprov}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Multiplicativity is not needed --- but boundedness is]\label{rem:nomult}
Bagchi-type universality holds for general random Dirichlet series, so the zero-free
alternative average comes from the \emph{full support of the chosen prior law} (its
covariance), not from any Euler-product or multiplicative structure. We keep the
coefficients multiplicative with \(X_2=1\) only for expository continuity; that would
matter solely if the argument \emph{relied} on the Euler product, which it does not.
\emph{Boundedness}, by contrast, is \textbf{not} dispensable: \(\|X_n\|\le 1\) is what
gives absolute every-\(\omega\) convergence on \(\Rea>1\) and so anchors the model to
\(\eta\) (\S\ref{sec:general}). So the requirements are ``bounded'' (critical) and
``rich enough support'' (universality), not ``multiplicative''.
\end{remark}

\section{The Bridge and the Assembly}\label{sec:bridge}

\subsection{Counterexample \(\Longleftrightarrow\) rcp-zero}

\begin{theorem}[The bridge]\label{thm:bridge}
For a suitable \(P\), isolating rectangle \(R\), and interior point \(s_0\),
\[
  \bigl(\exists n,\ \texttt{schoenfeld}(n+2657)=\texttt{false}\bigr)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \texttt{rcpZeroAt}(P,R,s_0).
\]
\end{theorem}
\textbf{(Lean: \texttt{counterexample\_iff\_rcpZero}; the single load-bearing
\texttt{sorry} of the route.)} The \((\Leftarrow)\) direction is the easy converse;
combined with the proved Theorem~\ref{thm:nondetect} it already gives ``no rcp-zero
\(\Rightarrow\) no counterexample''. The \((\Rightarrow)\) direction is the genuine
analytic content: a Schoenfeld counterexample, via Schoenfeld's equivalence and the
prior-model interpretation, forces nonvanishing conditional mass at \(0\), i.e.\ an
rcp-zero. The recipe (do not improvise) routes the counterexample through the
\(\Pi^0_2\) interpretation of the prior model; see Section~\ref{sec:unprov}.

\paragraph{The two conditional probabilities.} The bridge's content is Schoenfeld's
equivalence evaluated at the mean realization, written as two
conditional-probability-\(1\) statements:
\begin{align}
  \mathbb{P}\bigl(\text{counterexample} \,\big|\, X_n\!\equiv\!1 \ \wedge\ \exists s_0,\ \eta_X(s_0)=0\bigr) &= 1, \label{eq:cp1}\\
  \mathbb{P}\bigl(\neg\,\text{counterexample} \,\big|\, X_n\!\equiv\!1 \ \wedge\ \neg\exists s_0,\ \eta_X(s_0)=0\bigr) &= 1. \label{eq:cp2}
\end{align}
Conditioning on the null point \(X_n\equiv 1\) is legitimate because it lies in the
\emph{support} of the trace law (Tjur; the Radon/Mehler substrate) --- the surviving
content of the positive-mass lemma \texttt{priorBall\_edgeNbhd\_pos}. The counterexample
event is \emph{\(\omega\)-independent} --- a fixed arithmetical truth value --- and that
is the \emph{enabling} fact: it is why the interpretation \(\texttt{interpPi01}\) is
content-free (\(\mathrm{Iff.rfl}\); \S\ref{sec:unprov}) and why the prior law is free to
choose. Writing \(\mathbb{P}(\cdot)=1\) for these deterministic events is the Kopperman
move. Together \eqref{eq:cp1}--\eqref{eq:cp2} are exactly
\(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\Leftrightarrow\)RH (Schoenfeld) inside the rcp formalism; under the
\emph{limit} notion of \texttt{rcpZeroAt} (Remark~\ref{rem:placeholder}) they prove the
bridge genuinely, and the analytic input they consume is Schoenfeld (1976).

\subsection{Witness extraction: per-point to strip}\label{sec:witness}

The transfer ``no \emph{provable} zero at any \(s_0\) \(\Rightarrow\) no
\emph{provable} zero in the strip'' is \emph{not} the invalid schema
\(\forall s\,\neg\mathrm{Prov}(\varphi_s)\Rightarrow\neg\mathrm{Prov}(\exists s\,\varphi_s)\).
It is valid here for a specific reason: via the bridge, the strip existential is the
arithmetical \(\Sigma^0_1\) sentence
\(\exists n,\ \texttt{schoenfeld}(n+2657)=\texttt{false}\), whose matrix is a
decidable \texttt{Primrec} predicate. \(\Sigma^0_1\) sentences have the
numerical-existence (witness) property in \(\mathrm{RCA}_0\) over the standard model,
so a proof yields a \emph{concrete} \(n_0\), which decodes to a definite
\(s_0(n_0)\) in the strip --- and that point collides with the uniform
non-detectability of Theorem~\ref{thm:nondetect}. The \((\Rightarrow)\) proof
therefore begins with \(\exists\)-elimination on the \(\Sigma^0_1\) sentence to
obtain the \(s_0\) fed to \texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt}.

\subsection{Assembly}

\begin{theorem}[No Schoenfeld counterexample]\label{thm:nocex}
With \(R = \texttt{isoRect}\) the concrete rectangle
\([3/5,9/10]\times[-1,1]\subset\{1/2<\Rea<1\}\) and \(s_0 = 3/4\),
\[
  \neg\,\exists n,\ \texttt{schoenfeld}(n+2657)=\texttt{false}.
\]
\end{theorem}
\textbf{(Proved: \texttt{no\_schoenfeld\_counterexample}, modulo the bridge: it
rewrites by \texttt{counterexample\_iff\_rcpZero} and discharges the right side with
\texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt}. The hypotheses \(\Rea>1/2\) on the closure and
\(\eta^E_P\ne 0\) are supplied unconditionally by \texttt{isoRect\_re\_lo} and
\texttt{isoRect\_hAna} via \texttt{etaEulerApprox\_ne\_zero}.)}

\begin{corollary}[Schoenfeld sentence, then RH]\label{cor:rh}
\(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) holds \textbf{(proved: \texttt{RH\_PRA\_holds}, instantiating
Theorem~\ref{thm:nocex} at \texttt{isoRect})}; and, granting Schoenfeld's 1976
equivalence \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\to\) RH, every nontrivial zero of \(\zeta\) lies on
\(\Rea(s)=1/2\) \textbf{(\texttt{riemann\_hypothesis\_via\_rcp}; the analytic return
is the optional fourth \texttt{sorry} --- if the final statement is to remain the
\(\Pi^0_1\) form \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\), the development stops at
\texttt{RH\_PRA\_holds}).}
\end{corollary}

\section{Tutorial: the Kopperman Formalism and the
\texorpdfstring{$\Pi^0_1/\Pi^0_2$}{Pi-0-1/Pi-0-2}/RCA Model}\label{sec:kopperman}

This section explains \emph{why} the probabilistic substrate above is the natural
home for the argument, in the language of the \textbf{Kopperman formalism} shared
with the companion \(P\neq NP\) development. The punchline: non-detectability is the
``atomless measure does the work'' phenomenon, and the encode-then-fail-to-detect
strategy is exactly the statement that, in an \(\mathrm{RCA}_0/\Pi^0_2\) model, a
strip zero has no witness.

\subsection{A formalism is a triple}\label{sec:triple}

Following Kopperman (1967) and Mehler (1866), a \emph{formalism} for a piece of
analysis is a triple \(\bigl(\mathcal{H},\ \mathsf{Skel},\ \mu\bigr)\):
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a \textbf{separable Hilbert substrate} \(\mathcal{H}\) --- the ``territory'',
        whose existence needs the strong, incomplete ontological logic
        \(L_{\omega_1\omega_1}\) (metric completeness: every Cauchy net of
        approximants has a genuine limit);
  \item a \textbf{countable decidable dense skeleton} \(\mathsf{Skel}\subset\mathcal{H}\)
        --- the ``map'', finite combinations with rational data, on which all
        \emph{computation} happens, living in the reverse-mathematics base system
        \(\mathrm{RCA}_0\) (Recursive Comprehension);
  \item an \textbf{atomless probability measure} \(\mu\) on \(\mathcal{H}\) --- the
        device that, since we cannot algorithmically \emph{identify} which skeleton
        element approximates an undecidable target, lets us \emph{guess} one with
        full measure.
\end{enumerate}
``Choosing the measure \(\mu\) is choosing the model.'' In Lean the triple is the
\texttt{Formalism} structure (\texttt{PnpProof.Kopperman}); the RCP route builds its
instance \texttt{rcpFormalism} from \texttt{formalismOfPrior} applied to an atomless
substrate prior.

\subsection{The three layers in the RCP route}\label{sec:layers}

\paragraph{Substrate \(\mathcal{H}\).} The separable Hilbert space
\(L^2(\texttt{priorBall})\) --- \(L^2\) of the infinite product of the uniform
unit-disk law. Its existence and that of a basis are \emph{already guaranteed by the
Mehler formalism}, even in this \emph{non-Gaussian} (uniform-on-disk, bounded) case:
the Mehler construction together with the automatic separability of \(L^2\) supply the
substrate, with no need for Gaussian-specific Hermite/Zernike polynomials and no basis
to build by hand. So there is no ONB-construction, orthonormal-pair, or completeness
obligation. What we \emph{do} use is a \textbf{family of vectors for the rcp limit}:
the finite \textbf{indicator tensors} \(\bigotimes_n \varphi_{\alpha_n}\) (\(\alpha\)
finitely supported, \(\varphi_k\) indicator functions of subsets of the disk --- uniform
distributions on subsets of the original ball, trivial factors \(=1\)) that populate the
Tjur neighborhoods of \(e_\eta\) and carry the alternative averages. They need no
orthonormality or completeness --- only to be available as the vectors over which the
limit \(L = \lim_V \mathbb{P}(A\cap V)/\mathbb{P}(V)\) is taken. The one property kept is
\textbf{consistency to the original law}: the finite-coordinate marginals of these
indicator tensors reconstruct the original uniform-on-ball measure (their Kolmogorov
extension is exactly \texttt{priorBall}; the unconstrained coordinates always carry the
original \texttt{diskLaw}). The substrate is \textbf{Radon} --- a Polish inner-product
space, Borel measures inner-regular --- which is what makes the Tjur
limit~\eqref{eq:tjur} \emph{well defined and foundation-independent}. (In Lean:
\texttt{SphereGaussian.lean} for the Mehler substrate, \texttt{Measure.infinitePi} /
\texttt{infinitePi\_pi} for the consistency; the abstract atomless prior already in
\texttt{rcpFormalism} suffices.)

\paragraph{Skeleton \(\mathsf{Skel}\).} The finite Dirichlet approximants
\(\eta^E_P\) and the finite series \(\eulerB_P(\cdot,\omega)\) with rational data:
decidable, computably enumerable, the deterministic skeleton on which \(\eulerB\)
centers and against which \texttt{edgeNbhd} positivity is measured.

\paragraph{Measure \(\mu\).} An atomless Mehler-type prior on the coefficients, fixed
by its mean (\(\mathbb{E}[X_n]=1\)) and covariance (\S\ref{sec:general});
\texttt{priorBall} (the infinite product of the uniform unit-disk law) is one concrete
choice. Two properties do the work. \textbf{Atomlessness}
(\texttt{priorBall\_atomless}) forces detection to be a neighborhood (Tjur) phenomenon
--- no single realization, in particular the deterministic \(\eta\)-realization, carries
mass. \textbf{Full support} (universality of the law) is what supplies the zero-free
\emph{alternative average} that keeps \(L\neq 1\) in Theorem~\ref{thm:nondetect}; this is
Bagchi/Voronin and does not need multiplicativity (Remark~\ref{rem:nomult}).
\textbf{Bounded coefficients} (\(\|X_n\|\le 1\)) are independently required, for the
absolute \(\Rea>1\) convergence that anchors the model to \(\eta\)
(\S\ref{sec:general}). ``Choosing the measure is choosing the model'': different
admissible (bounded) covariances --- the unit-disk prior, the Fock-space pinning --- give
the same conclusion, because it is a property of \((\text{mean},\text{covariance})\), not
of any one law. (The bounded prior additionally gives the trivial \(r_0\) lower bound
behind the \(\forall k\) placeholder of Remark~\ref{rem:placeholder} --- a convenience,
not the load-bearing fact.)

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\Pi^0_1/\Pi^0_2$}{Pi-0-1/Pi-0-2}/RCA model
definition}\label{sec:unprov}

Inside a formalism, an arithmetical sentence is interpreted \emph{by ignoring the
model and reading its matrix over the standard naturals}; the model only fixes
\emph{which} probabilistic facts are available. Two hierarchy levels appear, and they
must be kept apart.

\paragraph{The \(\Pi^0_1\) sentence (the target).} \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) of
\eqref{eq:rhpra} is \(\Pi^0_1\): \(\forall n,\ p(n)\) with \(p\) the decidable
Schoenfeld matrix. Cloning the \(\Pi^0_2\) template one level down, the model
interpretation is
\[
  \texttt{interpPi01}(p, F, z) \;:=\; \texttt{Pi01}\,p \;=\; \forall n,\ p(n)=\texttt{true},
\]
a wrapper that \emph{ignores} both the formalism \(F\) and the \(\mathrm{ZFSet}\)
foundation \(z\). Its invariance \texttt{pi01\_invariant} is \(\mathrm{Iff.rfl}\)
(standard-\(\mathbb{N}\) absoluteness), and \texttt{interp\_schoenfeld\_eq} says the
interpreted truth coincides with \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\). This is the precise sense in
which RH, encoded à la Schoenfeld, is a \emph{foundation-independent} arithmetical
fact --- it needs no ZFC and no choice of measure to \emph{state}.

\paragraph{The \(\Pi^0_2\) witness (what detection would be).} A \emph{zero} at
\(s_0\), by contrast, is a \(\Pi^0_2\)/\(\Sigma^0_1\) \emph{witness} statement: ``there
is a realization exhibiting interior value \(0\) under arbitrarily good edge
agreement'' --- the \(\forall\varepsilon\,\exists\omega\) shape of
Definition~\ref{def:rcpzero}. The model reading turns this into a statement about
\(\mu\):
\[
  \text{``zero at } s_0\text{''}
  \;\Longleftrightarrow\;
  \rcp(\,\{\text{interior }=0\}\mid \text{edge}=\eta\,) = 1 .
\]
The Tjur limit \(L = \rcp(\cdot\mid T=t)\) \emph{exists} because \(\mathcal{H}\) is
Radon (Mehler/Gaussian-kernel structure inside the foundation-invariance layer), and
Theorem~\ref{thm:nondetect} gives the bound \(L \neq 1\) via universality
(Bagchi/Voronin): conditioning on a local edge neighborhood, a positive-mass family of
zero-free realizations keeps the conditional law off the point mass at \(0\). The
\textbf{load-bearing fact is \(L\neq 1\)}: a model proof of ``zero at \(s_0\)'' would
force \(L=1\); since \(L\) exists but \(L\neq 1\), ``zero at \(s_0\)'' is
\emph{unprovable}, uniformly in \(s_0\) (universality applies at every interior point).
No \(\Pi^0_2\) witness \(\Rightarrow\) the \(\Sigma^0_1\) counterexample search never
succeeds \(\Rightarrow\) the \(\Pi^0_1\) sentence \(\texttt{RH\_PRA}\) has no disproof.
This is the unprovability reading of the whole route, and it is the model-level content
of the bridge Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}. (The on-disk \texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt} instead
proves the stronger \(L=0\), but only for the trivial \(\forall k\) placeholder form of
Remark~\ref{rem:placeholder}, which carries no information about \(\eta\)'s zeros.)

\subsection{Fences (what the model does \emph{not} buy)}\label{sec:fences}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Restatement, not decision.} Casting non-detectability as
        \(L\neq 1\) does not by itself prove the bridge; both the
        \((\Rightarrow)\) direction of \texttt{counterexample\_iff\_rcpZero}
        (Schoenfeld) \emph{and} the limit form of \(L\neq 1\) (Bagchi) are genuine
        analytic debts. The formalism \emph{locates} them (two deep inputs) and
        certifies that everything else (the kernel, the prior, the convergence) is
        already proved or assembled from existing results.
  \item \textbf{No new axioms.} The Lean development stays within
        \texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}. The Radon
        substrate and the Mehler/bounded prior are the \emph{model in which the
        statement is interpreted}, not extra axioms; \(\texttt{interpPi01}\) is
        content-free (\(\mathrm{Iff.rfl}\)), so the \(\Pi^0_1\) truth carries no
        foundational commitment.
  \item \textbf{Only \(L\neq 1\) is needed; only unprovability is claimed.} The
        argument needs ``\(\eta\) cannot be \emph{forced} to a zero'' (\(L\neq 1\)),
        which universality supplies on a local neighborhood; it does \emph{not} claim
        ``\(\eta\) is probably nonzero'' as an analytic fact about \(\zeta\). The
        conclusion is that a counterexample is \emph{unwitnessable} in the model, not
        that there is no zero. (The on-disk \(L=0\) is only the \(\forall k\)
        placeholder; the substantive \(L\neq 1\) is the Bagchi load-bearer.)
\end{enumerate}

\noindent In short: the RCP route is the Kopperman formalism with
\(\mathcal{H} = \) the Radon substrate, \(\mathsf{Skel} = \) finite Dirichlet
approximants, and \(\mu = \) the atomless prior fixed by its mean and covariance; RH is
the \(\Pi^0_1\) Schoenfeld sentence, and its (unprovability) proof is ``a strip zero is
a \(\Pi^0_2\) witness, but conditioning on a local edge neighborhood, universality keeps
\(L\neq 1\), so there is no witness'' --- with two deep inputs, Schoenfeld (bridge) and
Bagchi (non-detection), and everything else \texttt{sorry}-free or assembled probability.

\section*{Status summary}

The probabilistic skeleton --- the random exponential Euler product, its never-zero
and free-Cauchy properties, the \emph{atomless} prior, the regular conditional kernel,
the positive-mass Tjur precondition, and the reciprocal contour convergence --- is
\textbf{formalized and \texttt{sorry}-free} (\texttt{RcpEuler.lean}), with the general
\((\text{mean},\text{covariance})\) convergence reusing existing project results
(\S\ref{sec:general}). The genuine debts are two deep, un-formalized analytic inputs:
\textbf{Schoenfeld (1976)} carrying the bridge \texttt{counterexample\_iff\_rcpZero}
(via the conditional probabilities \eqref{eq:cp1}--\eqref{eq:cp2}), and \textbf{Bagchi
(1981) universality} carrying the limit form of non-detection --- the on-disk
\texttt{not\_rcpZeroAt} proves only a trivial \(\forall k\) placeholder. Plus the
mechanical Schoenfeld matrix (3.1) and the optional analytic return to \(\zeta\) (5.3).
Read through the Kopperman formalism, the route says: a strip zero would be a
\(\Pi^0_2\) witness, but conditioning on a \emph{local} edge neighborhood, universality
keeps the Tjur limit \(L\neq 1\), so no witness exists and the \(\Pi^0_1\) Schoenfeld
sentence has no disproof --- an \emph{unprovability} conclusion, not a claim that
\(\eta\) is zero-free. Until these are discharged, the development is a \emph{verified
reduction} of the Riemann Hypothesis to them, not a complete proof.
